## What is it?
Port Forwarding is a special "rule" to be setup onto your router, allowing devices outside of your network to connect to your server. Whenever an application is receiving requests from a device outside of it's network, the port it is communicating on must be allowed through a port forwarding rule as to protect all of your devices from being "hacked". Port forwarding is a simpler cybersecurity system but it prevents most unwanted interaction with the rest of the internet.

The terms "opening" and "closing" ports have been coined for enabling and disabling port forwarding for specific ports. For example, most web servers communicate on port 80 or 8080 and their hosting servers must be configured to allow this traffic through the router so that end users such as you may communicate with them.
